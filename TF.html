Introduction

Team Formation (TF) becomes an important problem which has attracted a lot of attentions from the database research community. That is, given a collab- orative task with required skills and an expert social network, the team formation problem aims at finding a team of experts collectively satifying the required skills and communicating to one another in an effective manner.

Previous Results

Though this problem has been widely studied, these work propose different communication cost functions for discovering the teams such as graph diameter, minimum spanning tree of the team and so on. However, to the best of our knowledge, there does not exist a universal work to explore the positive and negative aspects of these proposed cost functions for forming teams. For example, we aim to explore the quality teams under the communication cost of graph diameter, minimum spanning tree, all pairwise distances, density of the subgraph and so on in different situations. Furthermore, it is also interesting to see whether the relationship between the team formulation with/without a team leader in the real-world datasets.

Results

We carefully implmented ten representative team formation algorithms which are RarestFirst, EnSteiner, MinSD, MinLD, MinDiaSol, MinAggrSol, MCC, ItReplace, LBRadius, and LBSteiner. All RAW RESULTS can be found in here or here. We compared the performance of these algorithms with respect to the number of required skills in the task based on five issues: (1) four types of communication costs of the team, (2) running time of team formation algorithms, (3) cardinality of the team, (4) workload of the team, and (5) personal cost of the team. We now present the task generation procedure as follows: For each task T, we first set the number of required skills k. We then continue to uniformly sample a skill s belong to S and adds it to the task T. After each sampling, we remove the sampled skill s from the skill set S in order to avoid the repeated sampling. For each configuration in the experiments, we repeat the task generation for 100 times and report the average performance of the team formation algorithms on these 100 generated tasks.
